Disease Awareness

Know Your Enemy

Understanding these three lifestyle diseases is the first step to preventing them. Learn the causes, recognise the warning signs.

Type 2 Diabetes
Diabetes

A condition where the body cannot properly regulate blood sugar levels, leading to chronically high glucose in the bloodstream.

Main Causes
  • Excessive sugar and processed food consumption
  • Physical inactivity and sedentary lifestyle
  • Genetic predisposition and family history
  • Obesity and excess body weight
Warning Signs
  • Frequent urination, especially at night
  • Excessive thirst and dry mouth
  • Unexplained fatigue and weakness
  • Blurred or deteriorating vision
  • Slow healing of cuts and wounds
  • Tingling or numbness in hands/feet

⚡ Did you know? Over 537 million adults worldwide live with diabetes and don't even know it yet.

Silent Killer
Hypertension

Also called high blood pressure, it's a condition where blood pushes against artery walls with too much force, straining the heart and blood vessels.

Main Causes
  • High sodium (salt) and fatty food intake
  • Chronic stress and anxiety
  • Lack of physical exercise
  • Smoking and excessive caffeine
Warning Signs
  • Severe headaches, especially in the morning
  • Dizziness and lightheadedness
  • Nosebleeds without obvious cause
  • Shortness of breath during mild activity
  • Chest pain or irregular heartbeat
  • Vision changes or blurring

⚡ Did you know? Hypertension is called the "silent killer" because it often shows no symptoms until serious damage has occurred.

Growing Crisis
Obesity

A complex condition involving excessive body fat that increases the risk of numerous health problems. BMI above 30 is classified as obese.

Main Causes
  • Consuming more calories than the body burns
  • High intake of ultra-processed and fast foods
  • Lack of daily physical movement
  • Hormonal imbalances and genetics
Warning Signs
  • Excessive weight gain and high BMI
  • Shortness of breath during simple tasks
  • Constant fatigue and low energy levels
  • Joint pain, especially knees and back
  • Difficulty sleeping (sleep apnea)
  • Increased sweating with minimal exertion

⚡ Did you know? Childhood and teenage obesity has tripled over the past 30 years globally.

Effects on Health

The Real Impact

Untreated lifestyle diseases lead to serious, life-altering complications that affect every system in the body.

01
Cardiovascular Damage
Hypertension forces the heart to work harder, thickening arterial walls and dramatically increasing the risk of heart attack, stroke, and heart failure.
02
Vision Loss
Diabetes damages the tiny blood vessels in the retina (diabetic retinopathy), potentially leading to partial or complete blindness if left unmanaged.
03
Joint Deterioration
Excess weight from obesity places enormous stress on joints, accelerating cartilage breakdown and causing chronic pain in knees, hips, and spine.
04
Mental Health Impact
Lifestyle diseases are strongly linked to depression, anxiety, and cognitive decline. Poor physical health creates a damaging cycle with poor mental health.
05
Kidney Failure
High blood sugar and high blood pressure together damage kidney filters over time, potentially requiring dialysis or a transplant in severe cases.
06
Shortened Lifespan
Combined, these three conditions can reduce life expectancy by up to 10-15 years. Early prevention in teenage years is the most powerful intervention.

Healthy Eating

Smart Diet Plan

Simple, delicious changes to what you eat can dramatically reduce your risk of lifestyle diseases. Here's where to start.

Go Green & Colorful
Fill half your plate with vegetables. Dark leafy greens like spinach and broccoli are packed with vitamins, minerals, and blood-pressure-lowering nutrients.
Fruit Over Sugar
Replace sugary snacks and sweets with fresh fruit. Apples, berries, and oranges satisfy sweet cravings while delivering fibre and antioxidants.
Hydrate, Hydrate
Drink 8-10 glasses of water daily. Swap sodas and energy drinks for water, coconut water, or herbal teas to cut hundreds of empty calories.
Whole Grains Win
Choose brown rice, oats, and whole-wheat bread over white rice and refined flour. Whole grains stabilise blood sugar and keep you fuller longer.
Healthy Fats
Nuts, seeds, and avocado provide healthy unsaturated fats that support heart health. A small handful of almonds beats a bag of crisps every time.
Cut the Junk
Limit fast food to once a week maximum. Ultra-processed foods are loaded with hidden salt, sugar, and trans fats that silently damage your body.

Daily Habits

Sleep & Screen Balance

Your habits outside the gym matter just as much as what you eat. Sleep and screen time have a profound impact on your hormones, weight, and mental health.

Why 8–9 Hours of Sleep?
▾
Teenagers need 8–9 hours of sleep per night. During deep sleep, your body releases growth hormone, repairs muscles, and regulates hunger hormones (ghrelin and leptin). Chronic sleep deprivation increases appetite, causes weight gain, raises blood pressure, and impairs insulin sensitivity — directly contributing to all three lifestyle diseases.
The 2-Hour Screen Rule
▾
Limit recreational screen time to 2 hours per day outside school work. Blue light from screens suppresses melatonin production, delaying sleep onset. Excessive screen time replaces physical activity, promotes sedentary behaviour, and has been linked to increased snacking on unhealthy foods. Set a "no screens 1 hour before bed" rule.
Stress Management
▾
Chronic stress elevates cortisol, which raises blood pressure, increases blood sugar, and promotes fat storage around the abdomen. Practice deep breathing, journaling, or spending time in nature. Even 10 minutes of mindfulness daily can significantly reduce stress hormone levels and improve overall health markers.
Consistent Sleep Schedule
▾
Going to bed and waking up at the same time every day — even on weekends — synchronises your circadian rhythm. This improves sleep quality, boosts metabolism, stabilises blood sugar levels, and makes it easier to maintain a healthy weight. Inconsistent sleep patterns disrupt hormonal balance significantly.
